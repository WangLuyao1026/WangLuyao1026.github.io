I am **Luyao Wang**, currently reading for an M.A. in **Journalism & Communication** (2025 Cohort) at [Nanjing University](https://www.nju.edu.cn/en/), specialising in **Computational Communication**. My research bridges data science and communication theory, with a particular focus on **user behavioural psychology** in digital platforms, the **social dynamics of information diffusion**, and the impact of **computational narrative** on public cognition and emotion.

### ðŸ§© Core Competencies: A Tripartite Fusion

My academic journey is structured upon a distinct "Practice-Data-Theory" framework, forming the unique cornerstone of my research:

* **Practical Foundation**: With a B.A. in Broadcasting and Television from [Jinan University](http://www.ujn.edu.cn/), I possess extensive practical experience in news media and omni-media platforms. My deep engagement in content planning, reporting, and convergence journalism has endowed me with acute insights into communication logic and user reception psychology.
* **Data Capabilities**: To transcend the analytical limitations of traditional media, I pivoted to Computational Communication. I have systematically mastered methods such as **Social Network Analysis (SNA)**, **Text Mining**, and **Statistical Analysis**, acquiring the capability to model and interpret large-scale user behavioural data.
* **Theoretical Vision**: Rooted in the intersection of communication and sociology, my research focuses on the interplay between data, users, media, and society. I aim to address critical issues emerging in the intelligent media era, such as "Emotional Contagion" and "User Psychology."

### ðŸ”¬ Research Interests

* **User Psychology & Behavioural Modelling**: Integrating psychological and sociological theories to explore mechanisms of information processing, emotional expression, and decision-making on social media.
* **Social Networks & Influence Diffusion**: Utilising network analysis to reveal propagation paths and key node effects regarding information, emotions, and behavioural patterns within complex social webs.
* **Computational Narrative & Public Opinion Evolution**: Analysing narrative frames and emotional mobilisation in multimodal texts (text, image, video) during public events, offering a computational interpretation of public opinion formation.

### ðŸŒŸ Vision

Returning to academia from the media industry represents a **"strategic pivot"** towards the future. It marks a critical leap from being a **practitioner** relying on traditional journalistic skills (legwork, observation, and writing) to becoming a **researcher** equipped with computational competence, communicative impact, and interpretative depth.

I aspire to build a bidirectional bridge between the "field" of traditional content production and the "laboratory" of quantitative data analysis through the deep integration of algorithms, data, and theory. My goal is to provide a perspective with greater explanatory power and humanistic warmth for understanding complex communication phenomena in the digital age.

I welcome collaboration with anyone interested in **User Psychology, Computational Social Science, and Data Journalism**.
