I am **Luyao Wang**, a Master's student (Class of 2028) in **Journalism & Communication** at [Nanjing University](https://www.nju.edu.cn/en/), specializing in **Computational Communication**. My research bridges data science and communication theory, with a particular focus on **user behavioral psychology** in digital platforms, the **social dynamics of information diffusion**, and the impact of **computational narrative** on public cognition and emotion.

### ğŸ§© Core Competencies: A Tripartite Framework

My academic journey is built upon a distinct "Practice-Data-Theory" framework:

* **Professional Practice**: With a B.A. in Broadcasting and Television from [Jinan University](http://www.ujn.edu.cn/), I possess extensive practical experience in news media and omni-media platforms. My background in content planning, reporting, and convergence journalism provides me with direct insights into communication logic and user reception psychology.
* **Data Capabilities**: To transcend the limitations of traditional analysis, I have pivoted to Computational Communication. I am proficient in **Social Network Analysis (SNA)**, **Text Mining**, and **Statistical Modeling**, capable of modeling and interpreting large-scale user behavioral data.
* **Theoretical Vision**: Rooted in the intersection of communication and sociology, my research addresses emerging issues in the intelligent media era, such as "Emotional Contagion" and "User Psychology," focusing on the interplay between data, users, media, and society.

### ğŸ”¬ Research Interests

* **User Psychology & Behavioral Modeling**: Integrating psychological and sociological theories to explore information processing, emotional expression, and decision-making mechanisms on social media.
* **Social Networks & Influence Diffusion**: Utilizing network analysis to reveal the propagation paths and key nodes of information, emotions, and behavioral patterns within complex social networks.
* **Computational Narrative & Public Opinion**: Analyzing narrative frames and emotional mobilization in multimodal texts (text, image, video) during public events, offering a computational interpretation of public opinion evolution.

### ğŸŒŸ Vision

Returning from the media industry to academia represents a pivotal transition from a **Practitioner** relying on "fieldwork and observation" to a **Researcher** equipped with "computational power and interpretative depth." I aim to bridge the gap between the "field" of traditional content production and the "laboratory" of quantitative data analysis, providing a human-centric yet data-driven perspective to understand complex digital communication phenomena.

I welcome collaboration with anyone interested in **User Psychology, Computational Social Science, and Data Journalism**.
